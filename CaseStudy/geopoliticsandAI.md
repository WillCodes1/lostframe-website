Prior projections, established in February 2026, anticipated a severe AI electricity crisis materializing by 2027 as grid capacities exhausted. President Donald Trump directly appealed to the Iranian populace in the early hours of the campaign, urging them to "take over your government". Instead of succumbing to revolution, Iran's Assembly of Experts rapidly initiated the succession process, moving to install Khamenei's hardline son, Mojtaba Khamenei, as the new Supreme Leader. This smooth transition indicated that the IRGC's grip on internal security remained absolute, effectively closing the window for a bloodless, citizen-led regime change.Shifting Timelines and the "MIGA" DoctrineThe absence of a civilian revolt has drastically limited the United States' strategic options, forcing a public re-evaluation of the campaign's duration and end goals. Initially, U.S. military planners projected that Operation Epic Fury would last between four to five weeks, echoing the contained nature of previous interventions like the capture of Nicolás Maduro in Venezuela.However, as the regime demonstrated operational resilience, continuing to launch retaliatory strikes against Israel and U.S. regional assets, the timeline became increasingly open-ended. On March 2, President Trump acknowledged the operation could go "far longer" than the initial projection. By March 7, the administration's rhetoric shifted from a targeted counterproliferation mission to a maximalist demand. President Trump declared on social media that the U.S. would accept nothing less than Iran's "UNCONDITIONAL SURRENDER!". Furthermore, he demanded the installation of "acceptable leaders" under a new geopolitical framework dubbed "Make Iran Great Again (MIGA)".This maximalist shift—requiring the total capitulation of a heavily entrenched ideological state—virtually guarantees that the conflict will be protracted. The administration's options are now severely constrained: it must either sustain a punishing, months-long aerial bombardment campaign to systematically erode every facet of the Iranian state, or it must introduce ground troops. While the administration initially denied intentions for a ground war, President Trump has increasingly refused to rule out the deployment of U.S. forces, specifically noting they might be required to secure enriched uranium stockpiles at damaged nuclear facilities.The Strait of Hormuz Calculus: Energy Control and Petrodollar HegemonyTo understand the administration's willingness to escalate into a prolonged conflict, one must analyze the broader geopolitical motives surrounding the Strait of Hormuz.
